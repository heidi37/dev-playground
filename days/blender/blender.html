Blender

I downloaded blender so I can make 3d models from flat images and possibly turn them into papercrafts.

The window is called the 3D Viewport

By default, a cube, a lamp and a camera are showing.

Hot Keys

It seems pretty critical to learn the hot keys quickly in blender.

[F12] - render

[G] - grab, tap X, Y, Z or G + hold middle mouse

[R] - rotate, tap X, Y, Z or G + hold middle mouse

[S] - scale, tap X, Y, Z or G + hold middle mouse

[esc] or right click - cancels the movement

[middlemouse - orbit, move view

[shift] + [middlemouse] - pan

[mousewheel] - zoom in and out

[ctrl] + [middlemouse] - smooth scroll, drag

[alt] + [middlemouse] - snap to nearest axis

numpad [period] - focus on any selected object

numpad [1](front), [3](side) [7](top) - align view to axis

numpad [5](in and out of perspective mode)

numpad [0] - look through the camera

shift + [D] - duplicate

[X] - delete

shift + [A] - add

[N] - properties of selection

[ctrl] + [A] - apply your scale

[~] - position short cuts

[o] - proportional editing

[tab] - edit mode

[alt] + [s] - move in the direction the vertices are facing.

[alt] + [r] - clears any rotation

[alt] + [g] - clears any movement

[r] + [x] + 90 - rotate 90 degrees on x axis

Notes

orthographic mode - a flat view, no depth in scene

mesh - to render anything you need to start with a mesh.

apply scale - things will work better in the future. Sets scale to 1.

In 3d rendering there is flat and smooth shading. Shade smooth is actually an illusion. It is not adding any geometry. The silhouette will still be jagged. You can see how high poly something is by the silhouette

Modifier(wrench) > Generate > Subdivision Surface - doubles the amount of detail across the entire mesh.

The points in the mesh are called vertices. A single point is called a vertex.

Proportional Editing - when grab, scale, rotate... grabs vertices around the vertice and moves together as a whole. By default the proportional size is really high. Click a vertex and scroll down.
